How It Started

Our very first messages were about small things – a missing video, a reel neither of us could find, voice calls and last‑minute plans. We didn’t know then that those tiny conversations would grow into something so big. The first “hey” on 22 Jan 2026 was the start of everything. You said you couldn’t find a video, I teased you, and a friendship started to blossom. Who knew a simple reel could lead us here?

On the night of 14 Feb 2026 we went out and I finally let my heart speak. I confessed what I had been hiding for weeks. You smiled, but you weren’t ready yet – and that’s okay. That night became one of our favourite memories: the walnuts, the laughter, the awkward “yo”, and the promise that even if it wasn’t the right time, we’d still be there for each other. This website is a love letter to those early days and everything that followed.

Things You Say That I Love

“babe”

You call me “babe” when you’re sleepy, annoyed, happy or just because. Each time, it makes me feel like I’m yours.

“baby”

When you switch to “baby”, I know you’re being extra soft with me. It melts my heart every single time.

“love”

You throw “love” around casually – in the morning, on calls, in texts. It never gets old. It’s my favourite word now.

“princess”

You are my little princess. Seeing you call yourself a “princess” always makes me smile; I love treating you like one.

“g”

Somehow “g” became our thing – part of almost every message. It’s casual, it’s silly and it belongs only to us.

“kiddo”

I rarely call you kiddo, but when I do, it’s because you’re being adorably stubborn. It reminds me how young at heart we both are.

A Letter I Never Fully Said

Dear Malaika,

I don’t think I ever told you the whole story of what goes on in my heart. I’ve tried, in hundreds of messages and voice notes, but words can only do so much. Still, here’s my attempt.

Thank you for letting me into your world, for laughing at my dumb jokes, for listening to my rants about cartoons and cars, for sharing your playlists and your silences. Thank you for being honest about your fears and for trusting me with your secrets. Thank you for teaching me that love isn’t always fireworks – sometimes it’s quiet patience, whispered apologies, and choosing each other again after misunderstandings.

There were moments when I thought I’d lose you – when denial felt easier than acceptance, when the weight of the world made you push me away. But every time, we found our way back. You’ve held me together when I was falling apart. You’ve let me be your rock when life was messy. We’ve both grown so much since that first “yo”. I’m grateful for every fight that turned into laughter, every tear that dried on your shoulder, every phone call that lasted hours, every nap I accidentally took while waiting for your reply.

I choose you – in the nights when we’re miles apart and in the mornings when we wake up side by side. I choose you through the “no’s” and the “yeses”, the arguments and the make‑ups, the long drives and the lazy Sundays. I choose you because my world makes sense when you’re in it. You are my favourite person, my home, my forever.

Yours always,
Rehman
